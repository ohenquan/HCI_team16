What is body mass index (BMI)?

The body mass index (BMI) is a simple and the most commonly used index used to classify overweight and obesity. It is defined as a person's weight in kilograms divided by the square of his height in meters (kg/m2). The World Health Organization (WHO) defines overweight as a BMI of ≥ 25 and a BMI of ≥ 30 for obesity.
